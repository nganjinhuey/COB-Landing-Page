In an emergency, go straight to hospital

The following conditions are not covered under COB and require immediate hospital care:

  • Chest pain suspicious of cardiac cause
  • Stroke symptoms
  • Severe breathing difficulty
  • Severe trauma or fractures
  • Loss of consciousness
  • Seizures
  • Severe bleeding
  • Acute abdomen
  • Pregnancy emergencies

Emergency & hospital care remains subject to pay-and-claim validation and medical necessity review.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Detailed answers to common operational questions.

What is Conditional Outpatient Benefit (COB)?

Conditional Outpatient Benefit (COB) is an outpatient healthcare support benefit provided to eligible WeKongsi Members in collaboration with HEYDOC Health. COB is introduced to provide Members with easier and more structured access to outpatient healthcare services such as telemedicine consultation, clinic visits, teleconsultation support, and nationwide panel clinic access.

The benefit is designed to improve outpatient healthcare accessibility while maintaining the long-term sustainability of the WeKongsi community.

Why is WeKongsi introducing COB?

Healthcare costs and outpatient medical utilization in Malaysia continue to increase over time. Through COB, WeKongsi aims to provide Members with better outpatient healthcare accessibility while ensuring the Program remains sustainable and community-driven for the long term.

Through collaboration with HEYDOC Health, Members will also benefit from improved healthcare support, wider clinic accessibility, easier outpatient access, and a more convenient healthcare experience.

Who manages the COB services?

COB services are managed in collaboration with HEYDOC Health, while WeKongsi remains the Program Administrator. HEYDOC Health will support outpatient healthcare services such as telemedicine consultation, outpatient episode management, panel clinic coordination, and teleconsultation support.

Who is eligible for COB?

All eligible WeKongsi Members who have completed the required waiting period will automatically be enrolled into COB. Eligibility remains subject to the Program Guidelines and applicable terms & conditions.

When will COB officially start?

The updated COB structure will officially take effect on 7 June 2026.

How do outpatient episodes work?

Outpatient episodes function as the utilization entitlement for outpatient healthcare services under COB. Members may use their episodes for telemedicine consultation and clinic visits based on their healthcare needs.

Episodes are distributed progressively throughout the year to support responsible and sustainable healthcare utilization within the community.

How many outpatient episodes will I receive?

Eligible Members will receive 2 outpatient episodes every quarter, with a maximum of 8 outpatient episodes per year.

The first 2 episodes will only be credited after successful completion of the required waiting period.

What is the waiting period for COB?

Members are required to complete a 90-day waiting period before receiving their first outpatient episodes under COB.

This waiting period is implemented to maintain fairness, sustainability, and responsible participation across the community.

What happens to unused outpatient episodes?

Unused outpatient episodes may be carried forward, subject to annual limits and Program terms & conditions.

Members are encouraged to utilize outpatient benefits responsibly based on actual medical needs.

What happens if I revisit for the same illness?

Follow-up consultation for the same diagnosis within seven (7) days will be considered part of the same outpatient episode.

This means Members will not consume an additional outpatient episode for follow-up treatment related to the same diagnosis within the specified period.

Will follow-up visits consume another episode?

No. Follow-up consultation for the same diagnosis within seven (7) days will not consume an additional outpatient episode entitlement.

Can I opt out from COB?

Yes. Members may choose to opt out from the outpatient benefit after registration. However, Members who opt out may lose access to outpatient healthcare support and related benefits provided under COB.

What happens if I want to opt in again later?

Members who choose to opt back into COB after opting out will be subject to a new 90-day waiting period.

After completion of the waiting period, the outpatient entitlement will restart fresh from 2 outpatient episodes. Future episodes will continue to be credited progressively based on the quarterly entitlement structure and Program terms & conditions.

What is covered under telemedicine?

Telemedicine services include online doctor consultation, medical advice, e-prescription support, and medication arrangement.

This allows Members to consult licensed doctors remotely without needing to physically visit a clinic for eligible conditions.

How much is covered for telemedicine?

Telemedicine services are covered up to RM50 per episode. The coverage includes online consultation and eligible medication support.

Any amount exceeding RM50 will need to be self-paid by the Member.

How do I receive my medication?

Medication may be either self-collected at participating Alpro Pharmacy outlets or delivered directly to the Member's address, subject to operational availability and service coverage area.

Can I choose self-collection or delivery?

Yes. Members may choose either self-collection or delivery for eligible medication support, depending on operational availability.

Which pharmacy is involved?

Medication support under COB is provided in collaboration with participating Alpro Pharmacy outlets.

What is covered under clinic visits?

Clinic visits are covered for approved outpatient diagnosis categories under the Program Guidelines. Members may visit participating MiCare panel clinics for eligible outpatient conditions.

How much is covered for clinic visits?

Clinic visits are covered up to RM120 per episode. Coverage includes eligible consultation and treatment charges within the approved limit.

Any amount exceeding RM120 will need to be self-paid by the Member.

What illnesses are covered?

Coverage applies only to approved outpatient diagnosis categories under the Program Guidelines. The approved diagnosis categories may be reviewed and updated from time to time based on medical and operational considerations.

Do I need to visit panel clinics only?

Yes. Clinic visits under COB must be conducted at participating MiCare panel clinics in order to utilize outpatient episode benefits.

What happens if my bill exceeds the coverage limit?

If the treatment amount exceeds the applicable benefit limit, the excess amount will need to be self-paid by the Member.

This applies to:

  • Telemedicine services exceeding RM50
  • Clinic visits exceeding RM120
Which clinics can I visit?

Members may visit participating MiCare panel clinics nationwide. The expanded panel network provides Members with access to more than 4,000 participating clinics throughout Malaysia.

Can I still submit outpatient reimbursement claims to WeKongsi?

No. Outpatient reimbursement claims through WeKongsi will no longer apply under the updated COB structure.

All outpatient services must be utilized through the HEYDOC outpatient episode system.

How do I use COB services?

Members will receive further instructions and guidance on how to access telemedicine services and clinic visits under COB.

Operational details, service flow, and healthcare access instructions will be announced progressively closer to the official rollout.

Will HEYDOC be integrated into the WeKongsi App?

Integration of HEYDOC services into the WeKongsi App is planned for future phases to provide Members with a more seamless healthcare experience.

Further updates regarding the integration timeline will be announced progressively.

How will I know my remaining episodes?

Members will be able to check their outpatient episode utilization through the service platform and future operational channels that will be announced progressively.

How do I find participating clinics?

The participating clinic listing and access instructions will be shared progressively before the official rollout of COB services.

Is COB cashless?

Yes. Eligible outpatient services are provided on a cashless basis up to the applicable benefit coverage limit.

Will I need to pay anything?

If the total treatment amount exceeds the applicable coverage limit, the excess amount will need to be self-paid by the Member.

What happens if my treatment exceeds the coverage amount?

Members will need to self-pay any amount exceeding the applicable outpatient benefit coverage limit:

  • Above RM50 for telemedicine
  • Above RM120 for clinic visits
Is there a monthly outpatient contribution?

Yes. COB includes a fixed RM15 monthly outpatient contribution to support outpatient healthcare services and operational sustainability.

Why is there a fixed outpatient contribution?

The outpatient contribution helps support outpatient healthcare accessibility, telemedicine services, panel clinic access, and healthcare operational support for the community.

This structure also allows Members to enjoy a more organized, sustainable, and accessible outpatient healthcare support system under the WeKongsi community.
